Responsive Redesign Assignment

The goal of this assignment was to practice redesigning a simple website. I was tasked with choosing an existing interface, analyzing and identifying flaws in the interface, creating low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for three screen sizes, and building a responsive website based on those prototypes.

Website

I chose to redesign a website for a restuarant called Luna De Xelajú. This is one of my favorite restuarants in my hometown. Although I had never explored their website before, I quickly identified multiple areas where improvements could be made. By enhancing their current website, Luna De Xelajú can effectively showcase their delicious food to attract new customers.

Problems I found:

  • It is difficult to see the photo behind their logo and information on the landing page
  • Their landing page has text in Spanish
  • Their address is not correctly spelled
  • There are no links to their Facebook, Twitter, or Instagram pages even though they include the icons
  • When you click on the Menu tab, you are taken to a different page where you don't immediately see the entire menu, it looks more like an ordering page

After putting their website into WebAIM WAVE, I found that the landing page reports zero errors and zero contrast errors. There are 37 aria labels and after reading them, I think some of the labels can be reworded for a more accurate description of the text and images on the site.

In my wireframes, I tried to include more pictures of the food and restuarant. I included a mock of a slideshow on the landing page to give new customers a quick look at food offered. I also included buttons to view the menu and order online. Following the original website's design, these buttons would take you to different pages of the site. I added a view menu button to make it easier for a user to navigate the site. This would also eliminate the original website's issue of only displaying the menu if you were online ordering. I also decided to display the location, hours, and contact information all in one section of the page whereas the original website has this information scattered throughout the page. Lastly, I put the restaurant's social media accounts in the footer, and I did not include Twitter because I could not find the restaurant's Twitter/X account, however, they are very active on their Facebook and Instagram accounts.
